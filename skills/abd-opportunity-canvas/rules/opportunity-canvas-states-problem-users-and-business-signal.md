# Rule: Canvas names customer problems, solution shape, and at least one business signal

**Scanner:** Manual review

The canvas is a frame for alignment, not a feature list alone. **Passing** means the document contains non-empty **`CUSTOMER_PROBLEMS:`** (segments and requirements), **`SOLUTION_FEATURES:`** (UVP / problem / major features), and at least one concrete business signal among **`KEY_METRICS_OF_SUCCESS:`**, **`REVENUE_DRIVERS:`**, **`COST_DRIVERS:`**, or **`KEY_ACTIVITIES_AND_RESOURCES:`**—enough that a sponsor sees why the moment matters. **Failing** means empty blocks, generic “everyone” segments, or only ideas in one row with no customer or value story.

## DO

- Write **`CUSTOMER_PROBLEMS:`** so a reader knows **which segments** and what **unique requirements** each has (role, situation, consequence for the bet).

  **Example (pass):** `CUSTOMER_PROBLEMS: Coordinators and dealer managers; coordinators need a single view of bay+tech when reschedules happen in under two hours.`

## DO NOT

- Replace **`CUSTOMER_PROBLEMS:`** with a product name or project codename only.

  **Example (fail):** `CUSTOMER_PROBLEMS: Project Phoenix` with no segment or requirement.

## DO

- Fill **`SOLUTION_FEATURES:`** with **UVP**, which **customer problem** you solve, and **major features**—not only a feature dump with no link to segments.

  **Example (pass):** `SOLUTION_FEATURES: UVP: slot you can rely on. Problem: three systems for bay status. Features: calendar + push on change.`

## DO NOT

- Omit **all** of **`KEY_METRICS_OF_SUCCESS:`**, **`REVENUE_DRIVERS:`**, **`COST_DRIVERS:`**, and **`KEY_ACTIVITIES_AND_RESOURCES:`** when the engagement is a real opportunity (workshop output should show how the org will know it worked and what money/cost/effort look like).

- Use segments as a vague **“customers: everyone”** when behaviours differ by segment.

  **Example (fail):** `CUSTOMER_PROBLEMS: everyone` for journeys that split consumer, partner, and internal paths.

**Source:** Agile by Design **Opportunity Canvas** (eight-row table) and training on problem, users, business stakes, and alignment.
