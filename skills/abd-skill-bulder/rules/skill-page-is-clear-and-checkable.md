### Rule: Skill page is clear and checkable

The skill page should explain what to do in plain language, and its quality checks should be explicit enough for a reviewer to accept or reject outputs.

#### DO

- Write sections in direct, grammatical prose with concrete actions.

  **Example (pass):** "Write one short purpose paragraph that explains why this skill exists and what outcome it enables."

- Keep rule statements decidable from the output being reviewed.

  **Example (pass):** "Each rule file includes `## DO`, `## DO NOT`, and pass/fail examples."

#### DO NOT

- Use vague guidance that cannot be evaluated.

  **Example (fail):** "Make it better and more professional."

- Leave placeholder text in final output unless explicitly marked as deferred.

  **Example (fail):** "{{CONCEPT_1_EXPLANATION}}" remains in a shipped skill page.

**Source:** Editorial convention for concise skill authoring.
