# Validated Learning — Plan / Validate / Learn (shared)

Shared across the idea-shaping skills. Every shaping artifact — an opportunity canvas, an impact map, a cost-of-delay estimate — carries assumptions: beliefs the case depends on that could be wrong. Rather than absorbing that uncertainty silently into a roadmap or backlog, teams make the assumptions explicit and confirm or refute them with the smallest useful check, updating the artifact as they learn.

## The loop

For each assumption worth testing, run a short cycle and keep the evidence visible:

- **Plan** — what to test: the smallest useful check, who owns it, and by when.
- **Validate** — run the check and collect evidence to confirm or refute the belief.
- **Learn** — what the team took away, and how the bet, the artifact, or the next experiment updates.

## When to reach for the full practice

This loop is the lightweight, shared summary. When a skill surfaces assumptions that must be tested before committing to build, use **abd-simple-validated-learning** to turn them into falsifiable hypotheses, prioritise a backlog of experiments by uncertainty and impact, and assign owners and explicit failure conditions.

Each skill keeps its own assumption *framing* (for example, impact mapping's build/outcome assumptions, or the opportunity canvas's Impact / Economics / Feasibility risk types) — only the Plan / Validate / Learn loop is shared here.
